The Engineering Problem

Standard CCTV is a Data Firehose

The Old Way: Dumb Recording

  • Cameras record terabytes of empty footage (a wall, a driveway).
  • Finding exactly when a person walked by takes hours of scrubbing.
  • Storage costs scale directly with time, not actual activity.

The AI Way: Too Expensive

  • Running heavy AI models 24/7 on video feeds destroys CPUs and kills GPUs.
  • Cloud-based solutions charge monthly fees and expose video privacy.

Abstracted Processing

Inside the Black Box

How do we make it fast and efficient? A smart, two-gate pipeline.

Gate 1: The Motion Filter

Uses extremely cheap, math-based pixel comparison. If nothing is moving, we drop the frame.

Cost: Nearly zero.

→

Gate 2: The Brain

Only wakes up when Gate 1 detects movement. Looks at the image to see if it's a person, car, or animal.

Cost: Used sparingly.

Result: The system sleeps 99% of the time, saving massive amounts of compute electricity.

Engineering Advantages

Why This Architecture Wins

Highly Efficient

By filtering out stationary video, we can run advanced computer vision on cheap edge devices like a Raspberry Pi or old laptop.

100% Private

No video ever goes to the cloud. All inference and storage stays on the local network physically under the user's control.

Scalable Design

Built with isolated parallel processes meaning a failure in one camera doesn't crash the whole system.
